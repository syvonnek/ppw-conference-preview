Location logos on the event tiles

Four ways to place each location's PPW wordmark on its own homepage tile. Each is shown on a dark photo (New York) and a bright photo (Mumbai) so you can see how it holds up either way.

The frosted "glass plate" keeps the full-colour logo crisp on any photo.

A · BareB · Plate top-rightC · Plate top-leftD · Above the title
A — Bare wordmark, top-right

Logo directly on the photo. Lightest, but note how it fades on the bright Mumbai sunset.

B — Glass plate, top-right

Logo on a frosted panel, top-right corner.

C — Glass plate, top-left

Same panel, moved to the top-left corner (opposite side from the date).

D — Logo above the title

Logo sits inside the text block, directly above the location headline — reads as a lockup rather than a stamp.

My recommendation: B or C (glass plate). On the bright Mumbai tile the bare logo (A) washes out; the plate fixes it everywhere. C (top-left) keeps the logo clear of the date on the right. D is elegant but the logo competes a little with the headline right below it.
